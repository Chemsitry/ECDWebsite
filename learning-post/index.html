Learning from children

Here is a sample of the writing I have contributed to our class written discussions.

Prompt: Why do you think teachers are so hesitant to provide children with direct instruction?

Teachers, especially if their instruction is not understood or misunderstood, might fear that their direct instruction causes them to be percieved as mean to the children. In this sense, I agree with Colten. I also think Hannah's idea that this might relect the child-centered attitude common in our society to be very insightful. According to the text, miscommunication is very common beause the children are very young, so I think this is a very valid concern. Colten also mentioned that this illustrates how adult-adult type interactions are not suitable for children. This aligns with the message of the text, but I think it also reveals a flaw of the text, as the author sets very high expecations on what adults should act and talk like to children. Some of these expecations are more relevant of a typical adult-adult interaction, over an adult child-interaction. Thus, there is a slight contradiction on the ways we are told to act by the text.

Personally speaking, I find myself somewhat hesitant to provide children with direct instruction. Given our discussion here, I'll try doing it more in the future.
